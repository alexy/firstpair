## About the book

A history of *ideas about* Russia and the Slavs, not of Russia itself: every major recorded encounter from the Byzantine homilies of 860 and Ibn Fadlan's Volga journal of 922 through the Muscovy travelogues, the Enlightenment, British Russophobia, race science, the Serbian case study, the Cold War, and the present — plus a five-chapter survey of the literary mirror in English, French, German, Slavic, and Nordic letters. The book extracts the recurring hostile tropes, traces each one's mutations across five centuries, formalizes them as an ontology of eleven trope families, and treats the modern accusation of "Russophobia" as itself a chapter in the history it names. Claims that passed a three-vote adversarial verification are asserted; everything else is flagged.

The full edition (eighteen chapters in twelve parts, three appendices, ontology, two research dossiers, complete bibliography and prompt log) lives in the book's source repository, which owns the manuscript, metadata, versions, and build logic; this site hosts only the public preview package.
